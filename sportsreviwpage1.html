Bronzy v:2.1

NBA Finals 2018,

Oisin Brick, June 7th, 2018
It is that time of year again where you start to ingnore everything else and listen to the sweet swishing of nets and deafening dunks of basketballs. And what better way to do that than to see the best players battle it out in the NBA Finals. But before I get into angthing, can we just appreciate the fact that the Golden State Warriors and the Cleveland Cavaliers made it into the finals for the 4th consecutive year? What was that you said? You are a Cleveland and or Warriors fan and even you are sick of see them in the finals? Yep me too. Although, I am a Houston Rockets fan myself. Houston Rockets R.I.P (2018 Westen Conference Finals) ;(.

The Stats


Okay. Now that we got that out of the way, lets talk about the Warriors and Cavaliers rematch. Just by looking at the standings, we can see that Golden State have a minor advantage with winning 8 more games. But that is not all. I think that we can say if we are 100% honest with ourselves that Golden State have so much more star power. But that is not to say Lebron won't put up a fight. As we all witnessed in game 1 when he dropped 51 points! But as we also witnessed, Cleveland losing the game. But it is unfair to say that it was The King's fault.(cough Jr.Smith). Regardless, I think Cleveland still has a chance. Because they do have the greatest player of our generation in Lebron James and agruably greatest ever.

There Will Be A Change....We Hope


Now that a couple of games have passed, it has become apparent as to who is running away with this series. I will admit that I did not see Golden State taking a 3-0 lead against Lebron's Cavaliers but the results dictate otherwise. Curry and Durant made sure of that. I mean, Curry was doing work the first two games with him scoring a median of 31 PPG and Kevin exploding with 43 points and 13 rebounds to give the Warriors a 3-0 lead despite Lebrons efforts. And I really do feel fo Lebron and Cleveland fans alike. I know everyone is still in hopeful spirits going into game 4. I hope they pull out at least one win. I don't think it would be deserving for Lebron considering his tremendous play thus far. though, that's just my opinion. But you cannot deny his stats. They speak for themselves. I mean, you have to look in awe when you realise that The King is averaging 38 PPG, 11 APG and 9 RPG! If you are not at least impressed by those stats i'm going to do it. I'm going to call you a "Lebron Hater".And all why he is 33 years old! Now we're just going to have to wait until game 4. Where either Lebron will pull out his first victory of the series or the Warriors will walk home with their 3rd NBA Championship in 4 years.
