Crushing It!: Gary Vaynerchuk,

Stephen Flynn, June 9th, 2018
The four-time best selling New York Times author is back. The author, of course, being the successful VaynerMedia CEO Gary Vaynerchuk, on January 30th of this year Gary released his much awaited follow up to the 2009 book Crush It!, a book about cashing in on your passion. Crush It! was met with great success not only giving Gary a bit more success in the path of becoming a great author but also providing many of the readers with the boost they needed to start cashing in on their passions and follow what they love to do.

This caused many of whom to become fairly wealthy in the process. This new book Crushing It! is very like Crush It! except for one big difference, this book is not about Gary's rise to fame or how he became the entrepreneur he is today but how the readers of Crush It! followed it and made a name for themselves, upon reading the book you may recognize a few big names that were either motivated to start because of Crush It! or played a factor in starting their success.

About Gary


Gary Vaynerchuk was born in Babruysk in Belarus on November 14th, 1975 currently aged 42 years old. His family immigrated to America where he noticed his love for “flipping” items (buying items for a low price and then re-selling them for a higher price), selling baseball cards and traveling across town to go to the yard and garage sales and buying items for low prices.

He then saw an opportunity in his father's liquor store and turned it into Wine Library, taking over his father’s business turning it from a $3 million dollar business to a $60 million dollar one. Now he runs VaynerMedia and a host of other businesses with the name Vayner attached to it, he was also an investor in many early startups like Snapchat also netting him a large return on investment. All of this work has earned him a large following on Facebook, Twitter, Snapchat, and Instagram, it is nearly impossible to see one of his grasping motivational speeches bouncing the internet from time to time.

About CrushingIt!


Crushing It! personally is one of the greatest accomplishment in Vaynerchuk's career, he managed to bring together a community of people that were all enlightened and inspired by him (as ego loving as it sounds), but what is more amazing is that maybe all those people wouldn’t be there, many wouldn’t have a home, children, a relationship or financial stability if it weren’t for the Crush It! book. Gary likes to call this a celebration of all those who achieved what they wanted from the book and here is the celebration.

At 263 pages the book is fairly short but packed information, there's nothing other to say rather than to read the book, but I will draw a quick conclusion, though this book is great and provides a much bigger platform to share the stories touched and moved by the story of Crush It! and possibly convince the next generation of entrepreneurs to make the leap this book was not as good as the original Crush It!. However, I would put it down to your preferred style of learning and take in information, personally the Crush It! book had a lot more content and provided a stepping stone of knowledge to use for yourself, but in total both books are amazing.

Who Does Crushing It! Help,


This book is a great example of how anyone can turn their lives around from injured NHL players to criminals who want to start over. The book speaks in my mind to everyone differently, some could be inspired, motivated or realize that this isn’t the thing for them, but it is impossible to finish this book without feeling different about entrepreneurship at the end. Summing things up this book is basically open to any and every category out there, everyone could at least use one bit of information from this book due to how insightful it is.

Best Parts


Crushing It! doesn’t exactly come with any gripping story and is very repetitive in a good way, the book was made from e-mail conversation with entrepreneurs who read Crush It!, and it helped them achieve their goals, don’t get me wrong each story is unique in its own way but they all follow a slight pattern, it’d start with a tough situation e.g bankruptcy, then notice an opportunity to make money, start small, read Crush It!, started crushing it, go big, then make it big.

Yes, this would be how many other small startups would make it big but to pick the best part would be the footnotes and characteristics of a business Gary has added, remember how I said that each story was unique in its own way? Well with the added characteristics and footnotes Gary points out what that one entrepreneur excelled at, e.g, providing content, time management, speed etc, they all correlate together.
